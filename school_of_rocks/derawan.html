School of Rocks

Derawan Berau Basin

Berau sub-basin is part of the Great Tarakan Basin, situated in the north of Kalimantan Island, geographically part of East Kalimantan Province, Indonesia. Recently, the basins are famous as significant coal producers in Indonesia, mainly from Miocene fluvial-deltaic sediment. The basin also has an active petroleum system indicated by several gas discoveries and many oil seepages around the city.
Numerous spectacular fluvial-deltaic sediments are widespread along the city and have become ideal outcrops for studying sedimentology and petroleum system features. The trip program combines modern and ancient environment observation of fluvial-deltaic and shallow marine carbonate while enjoying the iconic and memorable tourism objects along the trip.

OBJECTIVES

  • An introduction to regional geology, especially Neogene tectonic along the Kalimantan Island and their relation to significant fluvial deltaic environment development, the special case from the Berau sub-basin and comparing to Kutai and Great Tarakan basins.
  • Sedimentology exercise related to fluvial-deltaic and shallow marine sediment by comparing ancient outcrops and modern depositional environments.
  • Understanding the petroleum system component in the fluvial deltaic and shallow marine carbonate settings by observing outcrops analog, selected regional seismic lines and key wells along the basins to enrich the field discussion.
  • Exploring the nature, culture and cuisine of Berau, East Kalimantan Province

OBSERVATIONS
